Hello Everyone,

Now all of you would be planning to start preparing for placements and internships in some time. So thought of sharing some useful links.

This mail contains all stuffs that I could recollect, some of which were even shared by my seniors too, so credit goes to them too.

Few of the companies that visited our college this year are listed, so you may expect same:
  • Nvidia (Internship + Placement)
  • ARM (Internship only)
  • Analog Devices (Internship only)
  • NXP (Internship + placement)
  • STM (Internship Only)
  • E-infochips
  • Xilinx 
Subjects to focus on:
  • DC
  • DSD
  • DIC
  • MCA
  • MPA
  • Verilog (Palnitkar )
  • ICA (Not Full)
  • EDC 1/2 ( Not Full)
  • NA (Not Full)
Refer websites such https://www.glassdoor.com for reviews of above companies, types of questions etc.
Few interviews are telephonic only, specially if it is for only internship. For ARM it was a written test, followed by telephonic interview. For Xilinx Internship it was telephonic and for analog devices it was written at college followed by interview at company’s campus. So process may vary, and may or may not contain Aptitude. Still you need to prepare for aptitude too.

Red: High Importance, Blue: Medium,  Green: Low (For Core EC. not for coding related companies).

Digital Circuits

I did complete DC from Anand Kumar itself, many say Morris Mano but I never referred that book. It is entirely your call.

DC and DSD are most important subjects, even before VLSI. So be expert at state machines and related stuff. You might refer Charles Roth for DSD synch/asynch design, it is a good book.

Best link to revise Digital circuit concepts

Apart from Anand Kumar and Charles Roth, for DC revision and some good questions, read as much as you can do from below link:

http://www.exploreroots.com/ds23.html

For some puzzles and good questions (DC + VLSI mixed )

http://vlsi-soc.blogspot.in/p/puzzles.html

I am attaching a separate folder digital.zip which contains downloads from above two links only, but only those which I had read. That might be helpful if you do not have much time to browse all sub pages.

For VLSI basics

Entire Sang Mo Kang, as covered in DIC syllabus.

If you have time, then complete DIC syllabus is must. Still I am writing some important points, if you end up with DIC left on last day.

Chapter 3 ,5 and 6. (We did not saw any numerical based questions like some involved long iterative solving which were discussed in class, so no need of getting into that depth).
